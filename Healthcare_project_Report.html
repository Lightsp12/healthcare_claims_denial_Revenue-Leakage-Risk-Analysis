01 · Executive Summary

Claim Denials Are Costing This Health System $286.8M a Year

Claim denial rates remain elevated across the health system, prompting revenue cycle leadership to seek root causes and actionable solutions. Using SQL, Python, and a structured analytical framework, I extracted the claims dataset, conducted multi-dimensional denial segmentation, and quantified the total revenue impact.

The analysis revealed that nearly 1 in 5 claims is denied uniformly across all payers, resulting in a $286.8M gap between billed and approved amounts. I recommend the Revenue Cycle team take three immediate actions:

Appeal High-Value Denials

Launch a targeted denial appeal program for all denied claims above $5,000 — industry recovery rates of 45–60% put $86M–$115M within reach.

Front-End Verification

Implement eligibility verification and pre-authorization checklists before submission to eliminate preventable denials at the source.

Payer Contract SLAs

Negotiate explicit denial rate SLAs into all new payer contracts — the uniform ~20% rate across all three payers confirms this is a systemic, not contract-specific, problem.

Five Findings. Five Actions. One Revenue Imperative.

01

Denial Rate is Uniformly ~20% — Across Every Payer

Payer TypeTotal ClaimsDeniedDenial RateDenied Value
Medicare33,3606,72220.15%$68.86M
Medicaid33,3466,62419.86%$67.48M
Private33,2946,54819.67%$67.64M

The near-identical rates rule out payer-specific contract failures. This is a documentation and coding quality problem occurring upstream — before submission.

Recommendation Audit top denial reason codes. Deploy front-end eligibility verification and pre-auth checklists to cut preventable denials by 30–40%.
02

Reimbursement Model Offers No Protective Effect

ModelTotal ClaimsDeniedDenial Rate
Fee-for-Service50,0799,92219.81%
Value-Based49,9219,97219.98%

Value-Based contracts — often assumed to reduce administrative friction — show no measurable denial advantage here. The model type does not predict denial risk.

Recommendation Negotiate explicit denial rate SLAs (<12%) into all new payer contracts regardless of model type.
